Map

The main screen shows an interactive map. Your current location appears as a live marker that moves as you move. Tap the map to set your position directly, or open routes, favorites, and roaming from the buttons floating over the map.

Map imagery loads from tile.openstreetmap.org as you pan and zoom. Once an area has loaded, it's stored on your device, so it still works the next time you're offline.

  • Long-press anywhere on the map to open a sheet with "Walk here" or "Teleport here".
  • Tap the start button to begin from the current map position.
  • Use the re-center button to snap the camera back to your position after you've panned away.
  • Tap the search button to find a location by name and jump the map there.

Location search

Tap the search button (the magnifying glass) to open a search bar powered by an open location search service. Type a place name, address, or landmark, and matching results appear as you type. Picking a result drops a marker on the map and centers the view there. Only the text you type gets sent to the search service, never your coordinates or any identifying information.

  • Recent searches are saved on your device and shown as suggestions before you start typing.
  • Picking a search result does not move your location. Long-press the marker to walk or teleport there.
  • Typing coordinates directly, such as 35.6762, 139.6503, jumps straight to that point instead of running a search. It's a quick way to enter an exact spot.

Tap to move

Long-pressing any point on the map opens a sheet of options. What you see depends on what's happening at the time:

ActionWhat it does
Teleport here Your position jumps straight to the tapped point on the next update. There's no walking animation in between.
Walk here You move from where you are now to the tapped point at your current speed, in a straight line.
Walk here via roads Fetches a real road route and walks it a step at a time at your current speed. If the road route can't be fetched, it quietly falls back to a straight-line walk instead.
Add next point Only shown while you're already walking somewhere. It adds another stop to the walk instead of starting a new one. The first tap turns your walk into a chain of stops: where you started, where you were headed, then the new point. Later taps just add more stops to the end. If the walk was following roads, the new stretch follows roads too.
Share this location Opens your phone's share menu with a link to the tapped spot. See Share & Deep Links for details.

While a saved route is playing, the sheet shows different choices instead: Stop route and teleport, Stop route and walk here, and Finish route and walk here, which adds the tapped point to the end of the route instead of stopping it.

Routes sheet

Open your saved routes and start one straight from the map, no need to switch screens.

  • Tap a route to preview its path on the map.
  • Start, loop, or reverse the route without leaving the map screen.
  • While a route is running, you can watch your marker move along the path on the map.

Favorites sheet

Jump to any saved favorite location without leaving the map for the Favorites screen.

  • Tap a favorite to open its detail panel with three ways to get there: Set location (teleport), Walk to location (straight line), and Walk via roads (follows real roads).
  • Each saved favorite shows its name and how far it is from where you are now.
  • That distance is measured as a straight line, not the distance you'd actually travel by road.
  • Tap Save current location to add where you are right now as a new favorite, without leaving this sheet.

Roaming sheet

Set up automatic roaming and locationjoystick picks random spots within an area and walks between them on its own. This is handy for apps that expect you to keep moving without you having to steer it yourself.

  • Set a center point (your current position by default), a radius, and a total distance to cover.
  • Turn on road-following so each leg sticks to real walkable paths. If a road route can't be found, that leg falls back to a straight line.
  • Optionally have it walk back to the starting position once the distance is covered.
  • Each time it reaches a spot, it picks a new random one within the radius. Spots are chosen at random across the whole circle, not clustered near the center.
  • Roaming keeps running in the background, even with the screen off.
