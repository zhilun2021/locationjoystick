Tap to walk

Tap to walk gives you two shortcuts for moving your spoofed location with a single tap, without opening the app. Both are turned off by default. Turn them on under Settings → Menus → Tap to Walk.

Floating map quick walk

When this is turned on, tapping anywhere on the floating map starts walking to that spot right away. It skips the popup that normally asks which action to take, so the walk begins immediately.

Useful when you already know where you want to go and don't need to choose between Walk, Teleport, or Walk via roads every time.

Screen tap-to-walk overlay

This adds a crosshair button to the floating widget panel. Tap it once, and a clear layer appears over whatever app you're currently using. Your very next tap anywhere on the screen becomes your walk destination, and the layer then disappears on its own.

To cancel without walking anywhere, tap the close button in the bottom-right corner.

Scale setting

The overlay turns the spot you tapped on screen into a real-world location using the Scale value, shown in meters per pixel. A higher number means each pixel on screen stands for more real-world distance, which helps when the game map is zoomed out. A lower number works better for zoomed-in maps where precision matters.

If your taps are landing too far from where you meant, adjust the scale to match how zoomed in the game is. Zooming the game map out usually gives more accurate results, since small positioning errors matter less at that scale.

Important note

The overlay assumes the map you're tapping is oriented with north at the top. If the game rotates the map to follow your character, taps may not land where you expect. The Compass orientation feature below can correct for this automatically.

Some apps can detect when a location is being faked and may take action on your account. Only use this feature in apps where you understand and accept that risk.

Compass orientation

When this is on, locationjoystick takes a screenshot the moment you open the tap-to-walk overlay and looks for the red north arrow in the compass. It uses the arrow's direction to rotate the walk target so it lands in the right place even when the game map is turned.

Setup

  1. Go to Settings → Menus → Tap to Walk.
  2. Make sure Screen tap-to-walk overlay is turned on first.
  3. Under Compass orientation, tap Open Settings to open Android's accessibility settings.
  4. Find locationjoystick compass in the list and turn it on.
  5. Return to locationjoystick. The status updates to Enabled.
  6. Turn on Detect compass orientation.

Compass region

By default the app looks for the compass in the top-right corner of the screen. If the game you use puts its compass somewhere else, expand Compass region (advanced) in Settings and adjust the horizontal position, vertical position, and radius sliders to match where the compass sits.

Important note

Some apps can notice when this kind of background helper is active. If the game you use checks for this and limits features when it finds one, turn off Detect compass orientation, or disable the locationjoystick compass entry in your phone's accessibility settings.
